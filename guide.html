Getting started

How to read tarot

Tarot is a reflective practice. You are not predicting the future—you are listening to what the symbols illuminate right now.

1. Set an intention

Choose a clear, gentle question. Focus on what you can influence rather than demanding a fixed outcome.

2. Ground your energy

Take three slow breaths. Notice where your body feels open or tense before you draw.

3. Pull the cards

Use a simple spread—like Past/Present/Future—to keep the story readable and actionable.

Reading tips

Start with the card title and keywords to unlock the core theme.
Notice how the cards relate—do they support, contrast, or deepen each other?
Translate symbolism into real-world actions you can take this week.
Journal one sentence: “The message for me today is…”

FAQ

Do I have to be psychic? No. Tarot is about intuition and reflection, not prediction.
What if I pull a scary card? Every card has a lesson. Focus on the invitation to change or heal.
How often should I read? Daily for insight, weekly for trends, or whenever you need clarity.
